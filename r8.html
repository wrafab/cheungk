Week 7:Soldering/Electronics




















1. Capacitor - stores energy. (like a battery) 2. Transitor - used to switch electrical power. 3. Resistor - used to slow down or resist the electrical power. 4. LED - light emitting diode, emitts light. 5. Diode - a semiconductor that only allows energy to flow in one direction and not the other way. (polarised) 6. Potentiometer - Divides voltage. 7. Breadboard - the board where everything happens. Where you insert all electrical components and let the magic happen. 8. Multimeter - Electronic measuring instrument. 9. Switch - Diverts the flow of energy.
Voltage - a measurement of the pressure forced on the electrons travelling from the energy source. Current - flow of electrical charge. Resistance - Ohm's Law -The potential difference (voltage) across an ideal conductor is proportional to the current through it.The constant of proportionality is called the "resistance - The resistance of energy flow.

Ac - Alternating current - two way current of energy. DC - Direct current - one way flow of energy.
